Introduction

Water potability refers to whether water is safe for consumption by humans and animals. Potable water should not contain harmful levels of pollutants or microorganisms that can cause illness.

Testing for Water Potability

Water potability can be tested using a variety of methods, including chemical analysis and microbial testing. The World Health Organization (WHO) sets standards for water quality and provides guidelines for testing.

Common Water Contaminants

Bacteria, viruses, and other microorganisms

Chemical pollutants such as pesticides, heavy metals, and solvents

Natural substances such as arsenic and radon

Water Treatment Methods

To ensure water potability, it is important to treat water sources to remove contaminants and regularly test water quality. Drinking water treatment methods include filtration, disinfection, and distillation.
