What is Water Pollution?

Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies (e.g. lakes, rivers, oceans, and groundwater) by pollutants or toxic substances. These pollutants can be natural or human-made, and they can cause harm to aquatic life and human health.

Causes of Water Pollution

  • Industrial waste
  • Agricultural runoff
  • Sewage and wastewater discharge
  • Oil spills and leaks
  • Chemical spills and leaks

Effects of Water Pollution

  • Damage to aquatic life and ecosystems
  • Contamination of drinking water
  • Spread of waterborne diseases
  • Loss of recreational opportunities

Prevention of Water Pollution

  • Proper disposal of hazardous waste
  • Reducing the use of pesticides and fertilizers
  • Limiting industrial discharge
  • Implementing sustainable agriculture practices
  • Supporting laws and regulations to protect water quality
